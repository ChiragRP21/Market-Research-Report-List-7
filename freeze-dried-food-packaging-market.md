<p><p>The freeze-dried food packaging market is primarily categorized into rigid and flexible packaging. Rigid packaging includes containers such as jars, bottles, and cans, providing excellent protection and extended shelf life for freeze-dried products. In contrast, flexible packaging consists of pouches, bags, and films, offering lightweight, space-efficient options that are easy to seal and open. Both types cater to consumer convenience, with rigid packaging often favored for storage and presentation, while flexible options are popular for on-the-go consumption.</p></p>
