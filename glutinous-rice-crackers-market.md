<p><p>Glutinous rice crackers encompass various types, primarily Arare and Okaki. Arare are small, bite-sized snacks often seasoned and flavored, commonly enjoyed as a crispy snack or accompaniment to drinks. Okaki, on the other hand, are larger and typically thicker, created from grilled or fried glutinous rice and often have a crunchier texture. Both types are popular in Japanese cuisine and are enjoyed for their distinctive flavors and diverse seasonings, catering to various consumer preferences in the snack market.</p></p>
