<p><ul><li>Pressurized Tank Car</li><li>Non-pressurized Tank Car</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Railway Tank Car market comprises two main types: Pressurized Tank Cars and Non-pressurized Tank Cars. Pressurized Tank Cars are designed to transport gases or liquids under high pressure, such as liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) or chemicals, featuring robust construction to withstand internal pressure. Non-pressurized Tank Cars are used for liquids or semi-liquids like crude oil, ethanol, or food-grade products, operating at atmospheric pressure. Both types are critical for safe and efficient transportation of hazardous and non-hazardous materials across rail networks.</p></p>
