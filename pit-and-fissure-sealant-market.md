<p><p>The pit and fissure sealant market comprises primarily two types: resin-based sealants and glass ionomer encapsulants. Resin-based sealants are made from polymer materials, offering superior adhesion and durability, making them ideal for long-lasting protection against decay in permanent teeth. In contrast, glass ionomer encapsulants release fluoride, providing additional prevention against cavities while bonding chemically with the tooth structure. Both types are essential in pediatric dentistry, contributing to oral health by sealing the vulnerable grooves in teeth.</p></p>
