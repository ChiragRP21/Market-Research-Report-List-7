<p><p>The monosaccharide market can be categorized into various types based on the number of carbon atoms in their structure. Triose sugars contain three carbon atoms and play roles in metabolic processes. Tetose sugars have four carbons and are less common. Pentose sugars, with five carbons, are crucial for nucleic acids and energy metabolism. Hexose sugars, containing six carbons, are the most abundant, including glucose and fructose, and serve as primary energy sources for living organisms. These segments cater to diverse industries, from food to pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
