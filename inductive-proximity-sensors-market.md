<p><strong>The Inductive Proximity Sensors Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>NPN Inductive Proximity Sensor</li><li>PNP Inductive Proximity Sensor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Inductive proximity sensors are categorized primarily into NPN and PNP types based on their output configurations. NPN inductive proximity sensors output a low signal (ground) when triggered, making them ideal for applications requiring sinking current. In contrast, PNP sensors output a high signal (positive voltage), suitable for sourcing current. Both types are widely used in automation and industrial applications for detecting metallic objects without physical contact, optimizing efficiency and reliability in various machinery and production processes.</p></p>
