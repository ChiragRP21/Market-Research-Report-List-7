<p><p>The elbow prostheses market comprises two main types of materials: biomimetic and metallic. Biomimetic materials are designed to closely mimic the natural properties of human bone and tissue, promoting better integration and reducing the risk of rejection. They often offer enhanced durability and flexibility. In contrast, metallic materials, typically made from titanium or cobalt-chromium alloys, provide strength and longevity, making them suitable for high-load applications. Together, these materials cater to varying patient needs and preferences in elbow replacement surgeries.</p></p>
